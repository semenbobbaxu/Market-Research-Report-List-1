<p><p>The Germanium wafer market is primarily divided into two conductive types: P-type and N-type. P-type wafers have an excess of holes (positive charge carriers) due to the doping process with elements like gallium. In contrast, N-type wafers contain excess electrons (negative charge carriers) from doping with elements such as phosphorus. These distinct conductive properties cater to various applications in electronics and optoelectronics, significantly impacting the performance and efficiency of devices like transistors and solar cells.</p></p>
